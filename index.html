Hard Brexit 'may see Britain more than DOUBLE worth of failing EU agreements' say experts

A HARD Brexit could see Britain more than double the amount it currently earns while part of the failing Brussels bloc, experts claim.
By CHARLIE BAYLISS

Change Britain found 14 countries – including China, Brazil, India and Australia have publicly expressed a desire to strike a trade deal with Britain once Theresa May finally triggers Article 50.

The combined economic worth of the 14 nations who say they want trade deals with Britain amounts to £17trillion – more than double the £7.2billion of the combined GDP of nations who have deals with the EU.

The group – established by Michael Gove and backed by Boris Johnson – warned that Britain will only be able to take advantage of that potential should the Prime Minister pull us away from the customs union.

Secretary of State for International trade, Dr Liam Fox, is one of the key cabinet members who advocates leaving the customs union to give Britain more control of the trade deals it strikes.

Former CBI chief and trade minister to Gordon Brown, Lord Digby Jones, claimed such a move would open Britain up to “millions more customers”.
